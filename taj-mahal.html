Taj Mahal: A Symbol of Eternal Love

The Taj Mahal, one of the world's most breathtaking monuments, stands as a timeless testament to love, devotion, and architectural brilliance. Built in the 17th century by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan, this masterpiece continues to captivate millions of visitors every year.

üèõ The Story Behind the Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal was commissioned by Shah Jahan in 1632 in memory of his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal, who passed away while giving birth to their 14th child. Heartbroken by her death, Shah Jahan vowed to build the most magnificent mausoleum ever seen.

The construction took over 20 years and was completed in 1653. It remains one of the most stunning expressions of Mughal architecture, symbolizing both the grandeur of an empire and the depth of a husband's love.

The Taj Mahal perfectly blends Persian, Islamic, and Indian architecture, featuring a central dome, four minarets, and intricate calligraphy from the Quran.

üåç A UNESCO World Heritage Site

In 1983, the Taj Mahal was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site, recognized as the "Jewel of Muslim Art in India" and one of the Seven Wonders of the World. Today, it attracts over 8 million visitors annually, making it one of the most visited monuments on the planet.
