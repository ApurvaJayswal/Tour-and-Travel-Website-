Jaipur: The Pink City of India

Jaipur, the capital of Rajasthan, is known as the Pink City due to its stunning pink-colored buildings and rich heritage. Founded in 1727 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, Jaipur is famous for its magnificent palaces, historic forts, and vibrant culture.

üèõ History of Jaipur

Jaipur was established in 1727 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, a visionary ruler and an expert in astronomy and architecture. Before Jaipur, the capital of Rajasthan was Amber, located about 11 km away. Due to population growth and water scarcity, the king decided to build a new city.

Unlike many Indian cities, Jaipur was a planned city, designed according to a Vastu Shastra and Shilpa Shastra. The city was built with a grid-based layout, broad streets, and well-organized sections called Chowkris.

üé® Why is Jaipur Called the Pink City?

Jaipur got its nickname, The Pink City, in 1876 when Maharaja Ram Singh ordered the entire city to be painted pink to welcome Prince Albert, the husband of Queen Victoria. The color symbolized hospitality and has been maintained ever since.

üèØ Architectural Marvels of Jaipur

  • Hawa Mahal: The iconic Palace of Winds, designed for royal women to observe street festivities without being seen.
  • Amber Fort: A majestic fort with stunning architecture and breathtaking views of Maota Lake.
  • City Palace: The royal residence that blends Mughal and Rajput architecture.
  • Jantar Mantar: An astronomical observatory built by Maharaja Jai Singh II.
  • Nahargarh Fort: A scenic fort offering panoramic views of Jaipur.

üåç Jaipur as a UNESCO World Heritage Site

In 2019, Jaipur was declared a UNESCO World Heritage City, making it the second Indian city after Ahmedabad to receive this honor.
